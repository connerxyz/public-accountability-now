We have elected officials for a reason

Madison Dane County Board of Public Health continues to use emergency orders to dicate policy, while at the same time, insulating themselves from public comment or cooperation with local elected officials.

 

Emergency orders have a place. Sometimes authorities need to act too quickly to also provide transparency or welcome public scrutiny. But as the situation improves, it's a completely natural and expected part of the public process to begin requiring the public and their elected officials to approve further action.

 

The mission of Public Accountability Now is to legitimize PHMDC's authority by requiring a basic standard of public accountability. Swift action is required when dealing with any emergency. Involving the public to a greater degree is a positive sign of success, not a threat to public health.

So, what's the problem?

Members of Madison's Public Health Board have been under extreme pressure for more than 18 months. They've had to make some really hard decisions. (We can all relate right?)

 

In order to still get their job done, they've insulated themselves from the public and any critical consideration of their chosen policies. This has created a culture of defensiveness and dysfunction that does not align with democratic values or good government.

They censored public comment

Members of the public registered to speak to the board, their registration was confirmed. Yet, members of the board used technical procedures to block public comment on their mask mandate. They spent the rest of meeting talking about cats and patting themselves on the back.

Attack people personally

The board continues to attack people who ask for more transparency and public involvement personally. They label any information provided by the public as "misinformation". They're not capable of holdng themselves accountable.

Suspended the issue indefinitely

They used procedure again to not only ensure that the public was not heard but also to ensure the issue can't be brought up to them again. They voted unanimously to suspend the issue indefinitely.

They've immunized themselves from accountability, and this has created a dysfunctional process that needs to change immediately.
